## What counts as commercial use

You need a commercial license if you (or your organization) do any of the following with Guest Portal or a derivative work:

- Offer it as a **paid or ad-supported hosted service** (SaaS, managed hosting, MSP offering)
- **Sell, resell, or sublicense** the software, installers, or white-label deployments to customers
- Bundle it into a **commercial product** you charge for
- Deploy it **for paying guests or tenants** where Guest Portal is part of a revenue-generating hospitality or property business (hotels, venues, rental operators, etc.)
- Use it in any other way primarily intended to **generate revenue** from the software itself or from services built directly on it

Non-commercial homelab, personal Wi-Fi, charity, education, and similar uses described in the PolyForm license do **not** require a commercial license.

When in doubt, ask before deploying commercially.

## What a commercial license can include

Commercial terms are negotiated per customer and may include:

- Permission to use, modify, and deploy for commercial purposes
- White-label or redistribution rights (if needed)
- Support or update expectations
- **License fees and/or royalties** paid to the copyright holder
- Scope limits (sites, instances, seats, revenue tiers, etc.)

Each agreement is a separate contract; nothing on this page grants commercial rights by itself.
